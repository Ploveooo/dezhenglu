Riverside City Zoo Expansion Plan

Dezheng Lu

Professor Michael Lattanzio

MG201

September 14, 2025

Objectives/Outcomes

The primary objective of Riverside City Zoo expansion is to recover tourist attendance in zoo, increase visitor engagement, and make sure its long-term financial stability.

1. Financial Growth: Increase annual attendance by at least 25% within three years and boost ticket revenue by 30% through introducing new attractions and pricing strategies.

2. Visitor Experience: Enhance overall visitor experience by incorporating modern, interactive elements, providing a more immersive and educational environment.

3. Conservation and Education: Fully utilize self-advantages of being recognized for conservation programs and school partnerships, creating more interactive learning programs for children.

Premises/Assumptions

1. Public Support: Government and/or private sponsors are interested in investing this projects and providing a beneficial policies to facilitate the development, and families will be continuously expected to visit the updated zoo.

2. Economic Stability: The zoo's budget is stable, we can assume that utilizing stable income and budget can attract new capital injection.

3. Ethical Transparency: Animal rights groups will monitor expansion, requiring ethical and transparent practices.

4. Competition: Nearby theme parks and entertainment centers will continue to cause competition, but this zoo is unique in this area, and also provide meaningfully educational methods and conservation, which can create a distinct market position.

Alternative Courses of Action AND Evaluation

Option A: African Safari Exhibit

Develop a new exhibit featuring with giraffes, zebras, and elephants. It would include a new cafe and rest areas to support the new attraction.

Pros: The attraction is strong, iconic animals can boost attendance significantly, and new sponsorship opportunities.
Cons: The cost is very high, land is limited, hiring more professional personnel to take care of them.

Option B: Modernize Existing Enclosures

Upgrade existing animal habitat exhibit with interactive features, educational technology, and an immersive style of decoration.

Pros: Lower cost than new construction; improves animal living conditions; fully embody the importance of education.
Cons: The growth rate of tourist numbers is relatively small compared to introducing new animals, and requires temporary closures during upgrades.

Option C: Community & Education Center

Build a dedicated education hub with classrooms in which biology and veterinary medicine classes are provided, as well as set up some classrooms with 3D projection to introduce virtual/augmented reality wildlife experiences. This can stimulate children's interest in learning earlier.

Pros: Moderate cost; strengthens conservation and education mission; good for partnerships with schools, parents are more willing spend money to send their children to this zoo.
Cons: It may not be as attractive as introducing new animals, needs additional staffs and new program planning, and financial returns may takes a longer to appear.

Choice of Plan

For the short-term goal, I will choice Option B and Option C, because Option B will improve animal welfare and visitor experience, while adding Option C directly supports zoo's conservation and learning goals. This combined approach balances costs, addresses visitor demands, and strengthens the zoo's reputation. However, Option A may be considered as a long-term goal, if everything runs smoothly, funding increase and land available, this zoo can import some larger and iconic animals.

Supporting plans

Finance: Apply for public grants, approach local businesses for sponsorship, launch fundraising campaigns related to conservation education, expand memberships.

HR: Provide professional development for zookeepers and guides, recruit educators to run programs, and create volunteer opportunities.

Marketing: Launch a targeted advertisement and tell people about new zoo and get them to visit, partner with schools and family organizations.

Operations: Gradually invest according to funding and personnel, without affecting existing experience during renovation and upgrading process, and taking into account the ability to ensure accessibility for all visitors.

Follow-up Mechanism

Key Performance Indicators (KPI): Monitor monthly and annual attendance figures, ticket revenue, visitor satisfaction from surveys, and whether the educational project is successfully participated in or not

Regular Reviews: Hold weekly meeting, monthly meeting and quarterly summary to review KPI progress tracking table, and stage evaluation of the project.

Feedback Loops: Ensure timely feedback, keep stakeholders in the loop, and make continuous plan adjustment.

Incentive & penalties: Use formulating supportive plans to stimulate staffs to achieve goals, and apply penalties if targets are missed as well.
